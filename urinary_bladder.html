Kidney - Anatomy and Function

General Overview:

Kidneys are a pair of bean-shaped organs located on either side of the vertebral column in the posterior abdominal cavity.

Function:

  • Filter blood
  • Form urine
  • Maintain fluid and electrolyte balance

External Features of the Kidney:

    1.Shape: Bean-shaped
    2.Size: ~10–12 cm long, 5–6 cm wide, 3 cm thick
    3.Weight: 120–170 grams each
    4.Surfaces:

  • Anterior surface
  • Posterior surface
    • 5.Borders:
  • Lateral border – convex
  • Medial border – concave (contains the hilum)
    • 6.Poles:
  • LSuperior pole (near adrenal gland)
  • Inferior pole

  • Positioned between T12 – L3 vertebrae
  • Right kidney is slightly lower than the left due to the liver.

Blood Supply:

Renal Artery (from abdominal aorta) → Branches into:
1.Segmental arteries
2.Interlobar arteries
3.Arcuate arteries
4.Interlobular arteries 5.Renal Vein drains into the inferior vena cav

Internal Structure:

1.Renal Capsule: Outer fibrous covering
2.Cortex: Outer region containing glomeruli and convoluted tubules
3.Minor Calyces: Collect urine from renal papillae
Major Calyces: Formed by joining minor calyces

Nerve Supply:

1.From renal plexus
2.Sympathetic innervation helps regulate blood flow
